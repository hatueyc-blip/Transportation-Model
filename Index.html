Six non-negotiable strategic principles

1
Concentrate, don't spread. Deploy 40 buses on 3 routes and deliver an excellent service. A mediocre service on 11 routes fails — an outstanding service on 3 builds the brand and the case for expansion.
2
Coexistence, not displacement. GTC electric buses take new and express routes. NBA retains existing permits in Year 1. Managed transition begins in Year 2 with incentive-led retirement.
3
Charging infrastructure first. It MUST be ready, tested, and operational before the first bus arrives at port. This is the single most critical dependency — no chargers equals no operations.
4
Hire and train drivers now. The 90-day pre-arrival window is the only window to build the human capital required. A bus without a trained driver is a liability, not an asset.
5
Cashless from Day 1. Revenue leakage in cash systems is a known challenge in Grenada's paratransit. GTC must launch with digital payment from Day 1, even if a cash backup exists initially.
6
Dedicated Programme Manager required. A new or redeployed Electric Bus Programme Manager must be appointed. The CEO cannot absorb this workload into existing responsibilities.

Timetabling principles

  • Publish fixed timetables from Day 1 — fixed departure times build passenger trust

Charging infrastructure strategy

Critical path — longest lead-time item in the entire programme

Charging infrastructure must be ready, tested, and operational before the first bus arrives. GRENLEC may need to install a new transformer at the depot (3–6 months). The GTC CEO must write to GRENLEC within 5 days of plan approval.

Depot peak demand

330 kW

15 × 22 kW AC chargers simultaneous. New transformer likely required from GRENLEC.

Critical: Connector standard

Chinese buses typically use GB/T 20234.3 DC — different from the CCS2 standard used in Europe and the Americas. GTC must confirm this with the OEM in Week 1. Wrong chargers = no operations.

Solar PV opportunity

Explore a GRENLEC solar PV Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) for depot rooftop solar panels to offset charging costs. Install a smart CMS to stagger charging and avoid simultaneous peak demand.

06
Payment systems & fare structure

Phase 1 launch (Month 5): Dual payment

Cash to driver (paper receipt; deposited daily to GTC revenue account) + tap-to-pay NFC contactless bank card or mobile wallet at on-bus validator + QR code at door for app purchase before boarding.

Phase 2 (Month 6–12): Spice Card introduced

Contactless prepaid smart card available at terminals, supermarkets, and online. Discounted fare: EC$2.20 vs EC$2.50 cash (Zone 1). Monthly pass: EC$120 for unlimited Zone 1 trips. Student card integration with subsidised pass.

Month 12: Full cashless mandate

100% cashless on all GTC EB routes. Target: 50% cashless adoption by Month 6; 90% by Month 12. Data from cashless system feeds network planning and KPI tracking.

Fare structure (mirrors regulated fares)

Zone 1 — Grand Anse (adult) EC$2.50
Zone 6 — Grenville EC$6.00
Zone 5 — Sauteurs EC$6.00
Spice Card discount (Zone 1) EC$2.20
Monthly pass (Zone 1) EC$120
Sunday / public holiday surcharge +EC$0.50
Fares initially mirror existing regulated structure to avoid triggering Road Traffic Act Ministerial approval process for fare changes. GTC's value proposition is reliability and comfort — not price undercutting.

08
Coexistence with the NBA

The greatest threat to this programme is not technical — it's political

If the NBA perceives the electric bus fleet as a hostile takeover, they have the numbers, community ties, and historical precedent to mobilise effectively against GTC. GTC EBs launch on new capacity and express routes — positioned as an upgrade, not a replacement.

Months 1–3 Pre-arrival — fully cooperative

NBA status
Operates all routes as normal. No disruption to current operators.
GTC actions
Prepares infrastructure, recruits drivers, develops routes. Informs and consults NBA throughout.

Month 4 Soft launch — potential Zone 1 friction

NBA status
Continues all routes. Zone 1 private operators see new competition on Grand Anse corridor.
GTC strategy
Positioned as "additional service" not replacement. Aims to grow ridership rather than displace operators immediately.

Months 5–7 Phase 1 full operations — passengers choose

NBA status
Coexists with GTC EBs on same corridors. NBA older buses should NOT be displaced — they provide overflow and feeder capacity.
GTC strategy
Establishes brand; focuses on reliability and comfort advantage. Fixed schedules vs NBA fill-and-go model.

Year 2+ Managed transition — NBA transformed, not eliminated

NBA pathways
Feeder route permits; scrappage incentive for buses 15+ years old; driver priority recruitment pathway; subcontract model on rural routes.
GTC actions
SRO 43 vehicle age enforcement + scrappage incentives. NBA partnership model formally activated. Cashless payment data shows route performance.

Technology, software & suppliers

Core software systems

Transport Management System (TMS)

Remix, Optibus, Clever Devices, or SWJTU-recommended Chinese system. Real-time GPS tracking, route scheduling, driver performance, incident logging.

Cashless Payment Platform

WiPay Caribbean (ECCB-compliant) or Masabi Justride. NFC/QR tap-on-tap-off. WiPay strongly recommended for local support.

Passenger App (Spice Bus)

Real-time bus tracking, next bus prediction, payment, notifications. Can be white-labelled from payment vendor in Phase 1.

Charging Management System (CMS)

ABB Ability, Kempower, or bundled with charger OEM. Monitors charging status, energy consumption, smart scheduling to avoid GRENLEC peak tariff.

KPI Dashboard (BI)

Power BI, Tableau, or Metabase (free). Real-time dashboard for CEO and Board: ridership, revenue, fleet availability, energy consumption, punctuality.

Key supply categories

EV Bus OEM

Confirm manufacturer (Yutong, BYD, King Long, Higer, or CRRC). Secure service agreement: spare parts, technical training, warranty min. 3 years drivetrain, remote diagnostics access.

EV Charging Equipment

ABB, Kempower, BTC Power, or OEM-bundled. Confirm connector standard (GB/T vs CCS2) with OEM FIRST before any procurement. Issue RFP within 30 days.

Insurance

Massy United Insurance, NAGICO, or Lloyd's broker. EV hull insurance is a specialty market — engage broker with electric commercial vehicle experience.

Training Provider

TAMCC in partnership with Chinese OEM trainers on-site during commissioning. Establish fast-track CDL + EV induction programme jointly.

Maintenance / CMMS

UpKeep, Fleetio, or OEM's own system. Preventive maintenance scheduling, spare parts inventory, fault logging, warranty tracking. Chinese OEM may bundle.

Key performance indicators
KPITargetAction trigger
Fleet Availability Rate
% of scheduled buses operating on any given day. Daily (TMS). ≥ 90% Below 85% triggers mandatory maintenance review and fleet reserve deployment
Schedule Adherence / Punctuality
% of trips departing/arriving within 5 min of timetable. Daily (TMS). ≥ 85% Tracked per route and driver. Used in driver performance reviews.
Ridership (passengers per day)
Total daily passengers across all EB routes. Daily (payment system). +10% MoM (first 6 months) Reported to GTC Board monthly. Benchmark against pre-EB paratransit volumes.
Revenue per Bus per Day
Gross fare revenue ÷ operating buses. Daily (Finance). ≥ EC$800 / bus / day Below EC$600 triggers route review. Used to assess financial sustainability.
Energy Consumption (kWh/km)
Average energy use per km per bus. Weekly (CMS). ≤ 1.5 kWh/km Above 2.0 kWh/km flags maintenance or driver behaviour issue.
Unplanned Charging Downtime
Hours/week bus unavailable due to unplanned charging. Weekly (CMS). Planned overnight charging excluded. ≤ 2 hrs/week Bus pulled from route to charge = unplanned downtime event. Logged and reviewed.
Customer Satisfaction Score
Passenger survey rating comfort, reliability, cleanliness. Quarterly (app + paper). ≥ 4.0 / 5.0 Survey via app and paper. KPI drives service improvement budget.
Incident Rate (safety)
Accidents / incidents per 10,000 km operated. Monthly. ≤ 0.5 per 10,000 km All incidents reported to RGPF and GTC. Zero tolerance for preventable safety incidents.
Cashless Payment Adoption Rate
% of trips paid via cashless method. Monthly (payment system). 50% by Mo 6; 90% by Mo 12 Critical for revenue transparency and data collection.
Carbon Reduction Estimate
Estimated CO2 avoided vs equivalent diesel fleet (tonnes/year). Annual. Tracked annually Reported to GoG and UNEP/GEF e-mobility project. High PR/marketing value.

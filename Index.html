Six non-negotiable strategic principles

1 Phase 1 = GTC direct state operations — fastest path to December 2026 launch with full public accountability.
2 Phase 2 = Transit Grenada Limited (TGL) — PPP company (GoG 70% + private 30%) operational by Q4 2027.
3 Concentrate on 3 routes, not 11. An excellent service on Zones 1, 5 & 6 builds trust. Thin coverage across 11 routes fails.
4 Depot charging is the lifeline. No terminal upgrades before December 2026 — depot-only charging must be 100% ready by November 2026.
5 PSO subsidy is non-optional. At 60% load, Phase 1 is financially marginal without MoF subsidy — must be negotiated before launch.
6 Additive, not displacement. GTC EBs launch alongside NBA — Phase 1 does not displace existing operators.

04 · Charging Infrastructure
The Depot is the Lifeline

Critical constraint: no terminal upgrades before December 2026

Large terminal infrastructure works will not be complete before December 2026. Phase 1 charging strategy relies entirely on the depot (primary) plus targeted interim DC chargers bolted to walls at existing government or community facilities near route endpoints. This is achievable and does not prevent the launch — provided depot charging is 100% operational before the buses arrive.

CDL class confirmation

22-seater buses may require Class 2 CDL rather than Class 1. GTC must confirm with RGPF before advertising driver positions.

Critical on Grenada's hilly terrain. Drivers who achieve ≤1.2 kWh/km receive an additional EC$100/month efficiency bonus.

Shift structure

3 shifts for 40 buses = 55–60 drivers needed (includes reserves, rest days, shift overlap). Recruitment starts June 2026.

10 · NBA Coexistence Strategy
Additive, Not Displacement

The greatest threat is political, not technical

If the NBA perceives the electric bus fleet as a hostile takeover, they have the numbers, community ties, and historical precedent to mobilise effectively. Phase 1 GTC EB operations are clearly positioned as adding new services — not taking routes away.

Months 1–3 (Pre-arrival)

Fully cooperative — status quo maintained

NBA operates all routes as normal. No disruption. GTC prepares infrastructure, recruits drivers, engages NBA in consultation throughout.

Month 4 (Soft launch)

Zone 1 launches — additive, not replacement

GTC EBs positioned as "additional service". Aims to grow total ridership, not displace operators. NBA Zone 1 operators continue. Clear public communications essential.

Months 5–7 (Phase 1 ops)

Coexistence — passengers choose

GTC EBs run fixed schedules. NBA continues fill-and-go model. NBA older buses should NOT be displaced — they provide overflow and feeder capacity. Brand equity built on reliability and comfort.

Phase 1 specific coexistence commitments

✓GTC EBs operate on Zones 1, 5, 6 at fixed schedules; NBA continues on all their existing routes on these corridors
✓GTC does not invoke any exclusive route rights under the Road Traffic Act during Phase 1
✓No NBA operator is displaced, deregistered, or penalized due to the Phase 1 launch
✓GTC executes a formal MOU with NBA by November 2026 confirming these commitments
Year 2+ (Managed transition)

NBA transformed, not eliminated

🔀 Feeder route permits — formal permits with guaranteed minimum frequency and revenue

🔧 Scrappage incentives — for buses over 15 years old, linked to SRO 43 enforcement

👨‍✈️ Driver pathway — priority recruitment for NBA drivers with CDL + clean record

📋 TGL subcontracting — rural/low-demand routes TGL doesn't serve commercially
